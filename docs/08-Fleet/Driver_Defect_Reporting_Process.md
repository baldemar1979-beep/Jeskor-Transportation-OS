# Driver Defect Reporting Process

## Purpose

This document establishes procedures for reporting vehicle defects identified by drivers.

The objective is to ensure defects are reported, evaluated, documented, and corrected before they impact safety or operations.

## Reporting Expectations

Drivers shall immediately report:

* Safety-related defects
* Warning lights
* Tire concerns
* Brake concerns
* Steering concerns
* Air system concerns
* Electrical issues
* Fluid leaks
* Equipment damage

## Reporting Methods

Defects may be reported through:

* DVIR submission
* Phone call to management
* Text message to management
* Approved fleet management system

## Critical Defects

Critical defects require immediate notification and evaluation.

Examples:

* Brake failure
* Steering failure
* Tire failure
* Air leaks
* Safety system malfunction

## Non-Critical Defects

Non-critical defects shall be documented and scheduled for repair.

## Management Responsibilities

Management shall:

* Review reported defects
* Determine severity
* Coordinate repairs
* Verify completion
* Maintain records

## Documentation

Maintain:

* Driver report
* Repair records
* Vendor documentation
* Verification records

## Objective

Ensure vehicle defects are identified and corrected before creating safety or operational risks.

# Tire Management Program

## Purpose

This document establishes Jeskor Transportation's tire management program for company tractors.

The objective is to reduce roadside failures, improve safety, and control tire-related costs.

## Tire Management Philosophy

Tire issues are a major source of preventable downtime.

Drivers and management must identify tire problems early.

## Driver Responsibilities

Drivers shall inspect tires during:

* Pre-trip inspections
* Post-trip inspections
* Fuel stops when practical

Drivers shall report:

* Low tire pressure
* Visible damage
* Uneven wear
* Embedded objects
* Sidewall damage
* Tread concerns

## Management Responsibilities

Management shall:

* Review tire-related DVIR defects
* Coordinate tire repairs
* Track repeat tire issues
* Maintain vendor relationships

## Inspection Focus Areas

Review:

* Tire pressure
* Tread depth
* Sidewall condition
* Uneven wear
* Valve stem condition
* Visible damage

## Replacement Criteria

Replace tires when:

* Tread depth is below legal or company standard
* Damage creates safety risk
* Vendor recommends replacement
* Tire condition affects operational reliability

## Documentation

Maintain:

* Tire repairs
* Tire replacements
* Vendor invoices
* Repeat failure notes

## Performance Metrics

Monitor:

* Tire-related breakdowns
* Tire replacement cost
* Tire repair frequency
* Roadside tire events

## Objective

Reduce tire-related downtime and maintain safe tractor operations.

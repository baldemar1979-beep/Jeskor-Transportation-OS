# Amazon CAP Management Process

## Purpose

This document establishes Jeskor Transportation's process for managing Amazon Freight Partner Corrective Action Plans (CAPs).

The objective is to ensure timely response, effective corrective action implementation, and successful closure of identified compliance, safety, or operational deficiencies.

## CAP Philosophy

A CAP should be viewed as an opportunity to improve operations and prevent future enforcement escalation.

The objective is not simply to close the CAP, but to eliminate the root cause.

## CAP Lifecycle

### Step 1

CAP Receipt

Upon receiving a CAP:

- Review requirements
- Identify affected departments
- Assign CAP owner
- Establish response timeline

### Step 2

Issue Assessment

Determine:

- What happened
- Why it happened
- Impact to operations
- Risk level
- Root cause

### Step 3

Corrective Action Development

Develop actions that:

- Address root cause
- Reduce recurrence risk
- Improve operational controls
- Strengthen monitoring

### Step 4

Ownership Assignment

Assign:

- Action owner
- Due date
- Supporting resources

All actions must have a designated owner.

### Step 5

Implementation

Execute corrective actions according to established timelines.

Examples:

- Training
- Policy updates
- Process changes
- Additional monitoring
- Technology enhancements

### Step 6

Verification

Verify:

- Actions completed
- Controls implemented
- Root cause addressed
- Effectiveness demonstrated

### Step 7

Closure

Maintain evidence supporting:

- Action completion
- Monitoring results
- Improvement outcomes

## CAP Documentation Requirements

Maintain:

- CAP notification
- Root cause analysis
- Action plans
- Supporting evidence
- Completion records
- Closure documentation

## Escalation Criteria

Immediately escalate:

- Missed deadlines
- Repeated deficiencies
- Significant compliance findings
- Potential enforcement exposure

## Lessons Learned Review

Upon closure:

- Capture lessons learned
- Update JTOS documentation if necessary
- Improve monitoring processes
- Share findings with leadership

## Future Dashboard Recommendations

Track:

- Open CAPs
- CAP owners
- Due dates
- Overdue actions
- Closure status
- Repeat findings

## Success Metrics

A successful CAP demonstrates:

- Root cause identification
- Effective corrective action
- Reduced recurrence risk
- Improved operational controls

## Conclusion

Corrective Action Plans should be treated as opportunities to strengthen Jeskor Transportation's operating system. Effective CAP management reduces enforcement exposure, improves performance, and supports long-term AFP success.

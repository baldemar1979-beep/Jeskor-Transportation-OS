# Reasonable Suspicion Program

## Purpose

This document establishes procedures for identifying and responding to reasonable suspicion situations.

---

# Objectives

- Protect public safety
- Maintain regulatory compliance
- Support consistent decision making

---

# Reasonable Suspicion Definition

Reasonable suspicion must be based on specific, contemporaneous observations concerning appearance, behavior, speech, or body odors.

---

# Management Responsibilities

Only trained supervisors should initiate reasonable suspicion evaluations.

---

# Observable Indicators

Examples:

- Slurred speech
- Impaired coordination
- Unusual behavior
- Odor of alcohol
- Odor of controlled substances

---

# Response Process

## Step 1

Observe and document.

---

## Step 2

Consult management when appropriate.

---

## Step 3

Remove driver from safety-sensitive duties.

---

## Step 4

Coordinate testing.

---

# Documentation

Maintain observation records and testing documentation.

---

# Objective

Ensure consistent and compliant handling of reasonable suspicion situations.

# Driver Selection Framework

## Purpose

This document establishes Jeskor Transportation's philosophy for selecting driver candidates.

The objective is to identify drivers who will operate safely, embrace technology, represent the company professionally, and contribute positively to long-term operational success.

---

# Core Hiring Philosophy

Jeskor Transportation does not hire solely based on experience.

The company seeks drivers who demonstrate:

- Safety first mindset
- Accountability
- Professionalism
- Coachability
- Technology acceptance
- Reliability
- Physical capability
- Positive attitude

---

# Candidate Evaluation Hierarchy

## Tier 1 - Safety

Highest Priority

Indicators:

- Clean driving record
- Safety-focused interview responses
- Uses three points of contact
- Demonstrates situational awareness
- Takes ownership of mistakes

---

## Tier 2 - Reliability

Indicators:

- Stable employment history
- Good attendance history
- Consistent work performance
- Positive references

---

## Tier 3 - Coachability

Indicators:

- Accepts feedback
- Demonstrates humility
- Willing to learn new systems
- Open to process improvements

---

## Tier 4 - Technology Readiness

Indicators:

- Comfortable using mobile applications
- Comfortable using ELD systems
- Comfortable using camera technology
- Comfortable using electronic workflows

---

## Tier 5 - Professionalism

Indicators:

- Professional communication
- Respectful interactions
- Positive attitude
- Team-oriented behavior

---

# Candidate Risk Indicators

The following items require additional review:

- Frequent job changes
- Excessive preventable accidents
- Repeated policy violations
- Negative employment references
- Resistance to technology
- Poor communication
- Safety shortcuts

---

# Candidate Categories

## Preferred Candidate

Demonstrates strong safety habits, professionalism, accountability, and coachability.

---

## Development Candidate

Shows potential but requires coaching and structured onboarding.

---

## High-Risk Candidate

Demonstrates behaviors inconsistent with company safety standards or operational expectations.

---

# Final Hiring Principle

Hire for character and safety mindset first.

Train for processes and technology second.

Never compromise safety standards to fill a seat.

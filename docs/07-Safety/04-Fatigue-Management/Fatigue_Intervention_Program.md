# Fatigue Intervention Program

## Purpose

This document establishes Jeskor Transportation's fatigue intervention process.

The objective is to identify fatigue-related risks, intervene appropriately, and reduce fatigue-related incidents.

---

# Guiding Principle

Fatigue is a safety issue, not a disciplinary issue.

The primary objective is risk reduction.

---

# Fatigue Event Categories

## Low Risk

Examples:

- Single fatigue indicator
- Brief inattentiveness
- Minor alertness concerns

Response:

- Coaching
- Education
- Monitoring

---

## Moderate Risk

Examples:

- Multiple fatigue indicators
- Repeated fatigue events
- Observable drowsiness

Response:

- Driver contact
- Fatigue discussion
- Supervisor notification

---

## High Risk

Examples:

- Microsleep
- Eyes closed events
- Severe drowsiness indicators

Response:

- Immediate intervention
- Operations notification
- Driver removal consideration

---

# Intervention Process

Step 1

Review event.

Step 2

Determine fatigue severity.

Step 3

Contact driver if necessary.

Step 4

Document findings.

Step 5

Implement corrective actions.

Step 6

Monitor future performance.

---

# Documentation

Record:

- Driver
- Vehicle
- Date
- Event Type
- Severity
- Intervention Performed

---

# Program Review

Review monthly.

Identify:

- Repeat drivers
- Risk trends
- Intervention effectiveness

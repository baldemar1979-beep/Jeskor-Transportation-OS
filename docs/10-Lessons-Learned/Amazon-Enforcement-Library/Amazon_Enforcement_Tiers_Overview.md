# Amazon Enforcement Tiers Overview

## Source

AFP US Non-Compliance Enforcement Matrix

## Purpose

This document summarizes Amazon's enforcement tier structure and provides insight into how Amazon evaluates operational, safety, compliance, and business risks.

The objective is to understand how enforcement actions are categorized and escalated within the Amazon Freight Partner program.

## Enforcement Philosophy

Amazon evaluates both:

- Event severity
- Frequency of occurrence
- Repeat behavior
- Corrective action effectiveness

The enforcement framework is designed to encourage corrective action before formal escalation occurs.

## Tier One

### Description

Tier One violations typically represent lower-risk compliance or operational concerns.

### Common Characteristics

- Limited operational impact
- Correctable deficiencies
- Opportunity for early intervention
- Courtesy notifications may be issued

### Management Focus

- Immediate awareness
- Corrective action implementation
- Trend monitoring

---

## Tier Two

### Description

Tier Two violations represent elevated operational, safety, or compliance concerns.

### Common Characteristics

- Increased business risk
- Repeated deficiencies
- Escalating performance concerns
- Increased Amazon oversight

### Management Focus

- Formal corrective action plans
- Increased monitoring
- Leadership involvement
- Root cause analysis

---

## Tier Three

### Description

Tier Three violations represent significant business, regulatory, operational, or safety risks.

### Examples Identified

- Hours of Service compliance failures
- Unsafe vehicle operation
- Payroll funding failures
- Wage and benefit compliance failures
- Significant regulatory deficiencies

### Business Impact

- Corrective Action Plans
- Business Operations Reviews
- Growth restrictions
- Offboarding exposure

### Management Focus

- Immediate intervention
- Executive leadership involvement
- Corrective action ownership
- Continuous monitoring

---

## Key Lessons Learned

### Lesson 1

Amazon evaluates patterns more than isolated events.

### Lesson 2

Corrective action documentation is critical.

### Lesson 3

Leadership ownership increases as risk increases.

### Lesson 4

Business operations, not just safety, are evaluated.

### Lesson 5

Preventing repeat violations is more important than responding to individual events.

---

## Jeskor Transportation Applications

Future management systems should track:

- Violation category
- Enforcement tier
- Corrective action status
- Escalation level
- Repeat occurrences

## Conclusion

Amazon's tiered enforcement structure focuses on risk management, corrective action effectiveness, and organizational accountability. Jeskor Transportation should proactively identify trends, document corrective actions, and maintain strong operational controls to reduce enforcement exposure.

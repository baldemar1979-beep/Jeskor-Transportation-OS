# Tier Three Business Risk Review

## Source

AFP US Non-Compliance Enforcement Matrix - Tier Three Violations

## Purpose

This document captures key lessons learned from Amazon's Tier Three enforcement categories and identifies business risks that can threaten AFP participation, growth opportunities, and operational stability.

## Key Observation

Tier Three violations extend beyond safety and driver performance.

Amazon evaluates the company's ability to responsibly operate a transportation business.

## Major Risk Categories

### Regulatory Compliance

Examples:

- Hours of Service violations
- Driver qualification failures
- Regulatory non-compliance
- Required documentation deficiencies

### Vehicle Safety

Examples:

- Unsafe vehicle operation
- Vehicle condition deficiencies
- Failure to correct safety defects

### Payroll Administration

Examples:

- Payroll funding failures
- Delayed wage payments
- Payroll processing deficiencies

### Wage and Benefits Compliance

Examples:

- Wage compliance violations
- Benefits administration deficiencies
- Labor compliance concerns

### Operational Controls

Examples:

- Repeated operational failures
- Failure to implement corrective actions
- Weak management controls

## Lessons Learned

### Lesson 1

Financial stability matters.

Amazon expects partners to consistently meet payroll and operational obligations.

### Lesson 2

Compliance failures create business risk.

Regulatory issues can quickly escalate to enforcement actions.

### Lesson 3

Vehicle condition reflects management effectiveness.

Maintenance programs must be documented and actively managed.

### Lesson 4

Corrective actions must be implemented and monitored.

Amazon evaluates both the issue and the response.

### Lesson 5

Leadership accountability is expected.

Management ownership increases as risk severity increases.

## Jeskor Transportation Applications

Leadership should monitor:

- Payroll processing
- Driver qualification compliance
- Hours of Service compliance
- Fleet maintenance compliance
- Open corrective actions
- Regulatory exposure

## Future Dashboard Recommendations

Monitor:

- Payroll exceptions
- Compliance deficiencies
- Medical card expirations
- HOS violations
- Fleet maintenance status
- Open CAP actions

## Strategic Takeaway

Amazon evaluates transportation companies as complete business systems.

Strong compliance, payroll management, fleet management, and corrective action processes reduce enforcement exposure and support long-term growth.

## Conclusion

Tier Three enforcement categories highlight the importance of strong business controls. Jeskor Transportation should proactively manage compliance, payroll, fleet maintenance, and corrective actions to maintain operational excellence and reduce enforcement risk.
